Intelligent Contextual Legal Verification System

A Framework for AI-Driven Legal Traceability and Regulatory Consistency

Eduardo Luis Espinal de Aza, Esq. · Version 1.0 · October 2025

Executive Summary

The Intelligent Contextual Legal Verification System (ICLVS) is a public demonstrative research project in legal-tech. It outlines a conceptual architecture that applies AI-assisted reasoning to analyze legal documents, detect governing jurisdictions, adapt clauses to normative frameworks, and preserve audit-grade traceability—while retaining professional legal oversight.

Faster verification (conceptual) Fewer drafting inconsistencies Improved clause uniformity

Repository: github.com/eduardoespinal-lawtech/legal-verification-system

Project Description

ICLVS proposes a modular approach: an input layer for document ingestion (PDF/DOCX/JSON-LD), a processing layer for contextual legal rule analysis, an adaptation layer for clause revision against a jurisdiction-aware corpus, and a traceability layer for immutable audit records (optionally blockchain-anchored). The goal is to increase confidence, speed, and transparency in legal-compliance workflows across jurisdictions.

Use Cases

1) Regulatory Document Verification

Pre-submission review of bylaws, contracts, and filings by mapping clauses to applicable laws and standards.

2) Land & Real-Estate Registries

Assists attorneys/notaries by validating property documentation and registry-specific fields (parcel IDs, ownership chains).

3) Multi-Jurisdictional Contract Adaptation

Harmonizes agreements for multiple jurisdictions (e.g., DR, U.S., EU) without losing legal equivalence or traceability.

4) Financial-Sector Compliance

Supports compliance teams with automated clause screening for AML/privacy obligations and policy consistency.

5) Academic & Institutional Research

Provides a reference model to study explainable AI and rule-based systems in comparative law and RegTech.
